# WeSense Brand Guide

> Sense Your World. Share Our Planet.

---

## Brand Essence

WeSense is a community-owned environmental sensor network. The brand should feel **rooted in the natural world** while being **trustworthy and accessible**. We are grassroots scientists, not a Silicon Valley startup. The aesthetic is: *a field notebook meets open data*.

### Brand Personality

| Trait          | What it means                             | What it's not             |
| -------------- | ----------------------------------------- | ------------------------- |
| **Grounded**   | Earthy, honest, rooted in real data       | Not corporate or sterile  |
| **Open**       | Transparent, welcoming, community-first   | Not gatekept or exclusive |
| **Purposeful** | Clear mission, every element has a reason | Not trendy or decorative  |
| **Living**     | Growing, adaptive, connected to nature    | Not static or mechanical  |

## Dark Mode

Dark mode uses the deeper shades from each palette family. The key principle: **darken by shifting into the palette's own dark end**, not by inverting or adding grey.

## Logo Usage

### Current Logo

The current logo (globe with sensor waves) is marked as temporary. Until a final logo is designed, follow these rules:

- **Minimum size**: 32px height for digital, 10mm for print
- **Clear space**: Maintain padding equal to the height of the ".earth" text on all sides
- **Background**: Use on Warm White (`#FAFAF5`) or Charcoal (`#1A1A18`) only
- **Never** stretch, rotate, add effects, or change the proportions

### Wordmark

The "WeSense.earth" wordmark can be rendered in text:

- **"WeSense"** in Canopy Green `#2D6A4F` (bold)
- **".earth"** in Warm Grey `#8D8478` (regular weight)
- On dark backgrounds: "WeSense" in Fern `#52B788`, ".earth" in Pebble `#C7C1B7`

## Iconography

- Prefer line-style icons (not filled) to match the organic, hand-drawn feel
- Stroke weight: 1.5px-2px at 24px icon size
- Colour: Canopy Green `#2D6A4F` or Charcoal `#1A1A18` depending on context
- Recommended icon set: [Lucide](https://lucide.dev/) (open source, consistent with project values)

## Photography & Illustration

- **Photography**: Prefer natural light, real environments, real sensors in the field. Avoid stock photo polish.
- **Illustration**: If used, keep it simple, flat, and earthy. Use palette colours only. Avoid gradients.
- **Maps**: See *Data Visualisation Scales* below for map-specific colour rules.

## Data Visualisation Scales

Sensor data on the Respiro map uses **scientific standard colour scales**. These are separate from the brand palette and must never be modified — they carry established meaning that users and researchers rely on.

### Rule: Neutral UI Near Data

When brand UI elements sit adjacent to data visualisation (map panels, legend containers, control cards), the UI must use the **Neutral (Warm Stone) palette** — not the brand greens. This prevents the brand's Canopy Green family from blending with the green range of the temperature scale (0–25°C) or other data colours.

- **Legend containers**: Warm White `#FAFAF5` or Light Stone `#E8E4DD` background
- **Legend swatch borders**: Charcoal `#1A1A18` at 1px — works universally across light and dark data colours
- **Map control panels**: Neutral backgrounds only
- **Brand greens** appear in navigation, buttons, and headings — never in data-adjacent chrome

#### CO₂ (ppm) — De facto convention

No official colour standard exists. Breakpoints below are a consensus from WELL v2, RESET Air, ASHRAE, and the Aranet4/German CO₂-Ampel convention. Colours follow the EPA AQI palette for consistency.

| Range     | Category  | Hex       | Colour      | Basis                                        |
| --------- | --------- | --------- | ----------- | -------------------------------------------- |
| < 600     | Excellent | `#00E400` | Green       | RESET High Performance                       |
| 600–800   | Good      | `#92D050` | Light Green | LEED v5 Enhanced                             |
| 800–1000  | Moderate  | `#FFFF00` | Yellow      | WELL v2 threshold (~900)                     |
| 1000–1400 | Poor      | `#FF7E00` | Orange      | Aranet4 yellow zone; cognitive effects begin |
| 1400–2000 | Unhealthy | `#FF0000` | Red         | Aranet4/CO₂-Ampel red zone                   |
| > 2000    | Hazardous | `#8F3F97` | Purple      | Well beyond all standards                    |

#### Humidity (%) — No formal standard

No WMO, NOAA, or ISO standard colour scale exists for relative humidity. The convention below follows the dominant meteorological pattern: dry = warm/brown tones, wet = cool/blue tones (consistent with Windy.com and NOAA NDFD products).

| Range   | Category               | Hex       | Colour       |
| ------- | ---------------------- | --------- | ------------ |
| < 20%   | Extremely dry          | `#C8420D` | Brick        |
| 20–35%  | Dry                    | `#C2863E` | Amber        |
| 35–50%  | Moderately dry         | `#69AD38` | Yellow-green |
| 50–65%  | Comfortable            | `#38AEAD` | Teal         |
| 65–80%  | Humid                  | `#389DAD` | Blue-teal    |
| 80–100% | Very humid / saturated | `#384672` | Dark blue    |

> Convention source: Windy.com humidity colour scale

#### Pressure (hPa) — No formal standard

No WMO or ISO standard colour scale exists. Traditional synoptic charts use blue "H" / red "L" labels only. The scale below follows the OpenWeatherMap convention (the most widely documented implementation): low pressure = blue/cool, high pressure = red/warm, centred on standard atmospheric pressure (~1013 hPa).

| Range     | Category      | Hex       | Colour           |
| --------- | ------------- | --------- | ---------------- |
| < 960     | Very deep low | `#0073FF` | Blue             |
| 960–980   | Deep low      | `#00AAFF` | Light blue       |
| 980–1000  | Moderate low  | `#4BD0D6` | Cyan-teal        |
| 1000–1013 | Near standard | `#8DE7C7` | Light green-teal |
| 1013–1020 | Weak high     | `#B0F720` | Yellow-green     |
| 1020–1040 | High pressure | `#F0B800` | Orange-yellow    |
| > 1040    | Very high     | `#FB5515` | Orange-red       |

> Convention source: [OpenWeatherMap Map Legend](https://openweathermap.org/map_legend)

> **Source of truth**: All scale definitions live in `wesense-respiro/public/app.js`. Any changes must be made there first. The scales above should be implemented to replace the existing ad-hoc values.

## Tone of Voice

| Do                                       | Don't                               |
| ---------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------- |
| Speak plainly and directly               | Use jargon or buzzwords             |
| Say "we" — it's a community              | Say "our platform" or "the product" |
| Be specific: "PM2.5 readings" not "data" | Be vague or abstract                |
| Acknowledge complexity honestly          | Oversimplify or overpromise         |
| Use active voice                         | Use passive or corporate voice      |

**Example**:

- Yes: "Your sensor measures six things about the air around you. All that data is open — forever."
- No: "Our cutting-edge platform leverages IoT sensors to democratize environmental data."
